College Research

Biola Uno (Fall 2019)

In the fall of 2019, I was hired by Biola's Engineering Department and the Stewart Science Honors Program to research how to inexpensively build Arduino Uno's, which were dubbed "Biola Uno's." Using common circuit components and a microcontroller, I replicated the functions of an Arduino Uno. I also designed a PCB and manufactured over two dozen Biola Uno's using a CNC router and soldering components. My research was put together into a learning module, which is used as a lab in Biola's Intro to Digital Circuits (PHSC 322). During the Spring of 2020, I presented a guest lecture to introduce my research and the lab to the class. Moving forward, the Biola Uno is going to be used for further projects, research, and produced as gifts for prospective students and donors.

My research was accepted for publication at two conferences. At the 2019 Southern California Conference for Undergraduate Research, I published and presented a poster, Uno by Biola, Bridging the Gap Between Analog and Digital Circuits in Engineering Curriculum." At the 2020 American Society for Engineering Education PSW Conference, I published a full paper, "The Biola Uno Project, A Cost-friendly and Experience-enriching Learning Module for Introductory Embedded Systems."

Biola's Smart Garden (Summer 2018)

In the summer of 2018, I was hired by Biola's Engineering Department to partner with Biola's Biology Department and work on Biola's Organic Garden. I was tasked with designing a system to control the sprinklers in the garden and to interface with sensors that maintained the fish pond. I developed a system using a Raspberry Pi 3 and Python to control the Organic Garden. The system alternates between watering four sprinkler beds. The system checks disolved oxygen, temperature, and water level of the tank and turns on appropriate sprinklers when necessary to correct issues. All processes are logged and can be accessed through a virtual monitor and VNC connect. The system interfaces with Twitter to tweet whenever a change to the sprinkler system has been made so that the system can provide easy notifications. Also, the Smart Garden is connected to Alexa, allowing quick status updates by asking, "Alexa, how is my garden doing?"
