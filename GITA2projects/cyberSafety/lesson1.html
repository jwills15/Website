Basic Tips for Social Media Privacy

  • Social media is a platform in which you present an image of yourself. Your profile and postings are available to everyone, so you should appear the way you wish others to perceive yourself.
  • In addition, your posts should be respectful.
  • Donâ€™t rashly upload anything you may regret.
  • Use privacy in which only friends can view your profile, not friends of friends, and accept friend requests manually, not automatically.
  • Remember, anything you post to social media is permanent and can always be found, so be careful.

Beware of Catfishing

  • A catfish is someone who pretends to be someone they're not using a social media platform to create false identities, especially to pursue younger people.
  • They usually ask too many questions and are too serious too soon.
  • Most catfishers can be discovered by taking the profile picture and using it to search Google. If a model or celebrity comes up as a result, then the account is most likely a catfisher.
  • Most social media websites have verified the identity of popular figures so that people will know which account is the real one.
  • Be cautious of fake accounts. Try to verify the identity of another user before interacting with them.
