Ashley Madison Hack: Social Implications

What is Ashley Madison?

Ashley Madison Facts:
Ashely Madison was a site for having affairs with over 40 million users. They prided themselves on security and the ability to enable affairs without consequences.
The Hack
A group called "The Impact Team" hacked Ashley Madison in July 2015, and threatened to release the data unless the website was shut down. When this did not happen, on August 18 the hackers released over 60 gigabytes of user and company data on the dark web, which eventually made its way onto the open web.
The Aftermath
Ashley Madison still remains online, but is no longer trusted by users. The CEO of the company was forced out after the hack, and the hackers are being pursued by legal authorities.

What are the implications for everyone on the internet?

  • Shows the inability of any website to provide 100% guaranteed protection (like Ashley Madison did)
  • Reveals the necessity of strong passwords for users
  • Shows that everything put on the internet can be accessed by anyone
  • Hackers can access anything they want with little to no evidence and ruin people's lives
  • Never trust a website to keep your information safe

What are the consequences for users of Ashley Madison?

Web developers created a website to find if a person had used Ashley Madison, simply by entering the person's name and email address. This has revealed many people who cheated on their spouse, and has caused several suicides. Credit card information was also released, along with passwords of users, which showed that most passwords were "123456" or "password," which are very easy to break.

Beware on the internet! You don't know what might get out there...
