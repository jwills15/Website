Social Effects of Social Media

  • A large part of this generationâ€™s social and emotional development is occurring while on the Internet and over texts, which makes communication with real people harder.
  • Limit the amount of time spent texting or interacting on social media, and try to call or meet in person if that is possible.
  • Also, spending time with family and being present with people must be made a priority.
  • Social media can also lead to cyber bullying.
  • If you are being bullied, talk to your parents or another important adult and solve the problem. Don't just suffer in silence.

Social Media is Very Addictive

  • 92% of teens say they have to check social media at least once every day, while many are online constantly or for hours at a time.
  • Being on social media puts users at risk of being addicted and wasting time, which could be better used for school or physical activity.
  • Set time limits for how much time you want to spend or use programs which block social media sites after a certain amount of time.
  • Be aware of how much time you are spending. The first step to solving a problem is realizing you have a problem.
