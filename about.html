About Me Joshua Williams

College

I am a computer engineering student at the University of Southern California. I originally studied at Biola University as a part of the 3+2 program. At the end of the program, I will have a BS in Engineering-Phyics and a minor in Bible from Biola, and a BS in Computer Engineering and Computer Science with an emphasis in embedded systems from USC.

At Biola, I finished with a 4.0 GPA. I was a teaching assistant for six classes and involved in two major on-campus research projects, resulting in two publications.

Professional Experience

My professoinal engineering career began when I interned at Boeing Huntington Beach during the summer of 2016. I was an electrical engineering intern and was mentored by a senior Boeing electrical engineer. It was a great experience and began my passion for engineering. I worked on many amazing projects that made me excited to go to work every day. Among my responsibilities, I helped my mentor test circuit boards for the Space Launch System (SLS) program, I helped organize a Preliminary Design Review (PDR) for the Patriot Advanced Capability-3 (PAC-3) program, and I used my computer science knowledge to add features to a company-specific lab report program written in C#.

During the summer of 2017, I was invited back to Boeing Huntington Beach to participate in the BEAM program, which is an externship where I was able to network, learn about Boeing and engineering careers, and compete in several team design challenges. The program was populated by a small group of underclassmen applicants from local universities, as well as a few former interns that were invited back after the previous summer. I was very happy to be invited back to such a select program and had an amazing time continuing to explore engineering.

For the first half of the summer of 2018, I was hired by Biola's engnineering department to participate in research. I was chosen to work alongside a Biology professor to create a system to control Biola's Organic Garden. You can read more about the project on my research page (here).

For the second half of the summer of 2018, I was hired by Pioneer Circuits Inc. as an engineering intern. I worked alongside the engineering department to improve efficiency and organization. I was tasked with performing data analysis to adjust the scales used in producing boards, and I was able to improve size accuracy by 50%. I also created and organized a database of engineering specification documents necessary for the production and quality assurance of circuit boards.

In the summer of 2019, I worked as a software engineering intern at Liferay, Inc. Liferay produces an open-source digital experience platform. As an intern, I received extensive training Java/JSP back-end development and in Liferay's DXP Cloud. After completing my training, I was assigned to work on portlets for an internal department database portal. I developed a custom CSV import portlet which is used to perform a weekly database update of the department's clients and projects. Also, I added features to a custom full-stack staff display portlet for the database's GUI. My internship taught me how to work as a team to write software using Github, code reviews, and AGILE development. After completing my internship, I received a return offer to intern during the summer of 2020, but, unfortunately, it was canceled due to COVID-19.

In the fall of 2019, I was hired to research building Arduino Uno's for Biola University. My research was published at the 2019 SCCUR conference and the 2020 ASEE PSW conference. You can read more about my publications on my research page (here).

High School

In 2017, I graduated from Brea Olinda High School (BOHS). I was valedictorian with a 4.63 GPA. I also received the National AP Scholar award; I took 12 AP tests and received a 5 on all of them, highlighted by AP Calculus BC, AP Computer Science, AP Physics 1, and AP Physics 2. At BOHS, I was a part of the Global Information Technology Academy (GITA) program, which is a track of computer science courses. I took three years of computer programming in high school through this program, becoming proficient in C#, HTML, CSS, Javascript, and Java.

At BOHS, I was involved in the National Honor Society (NHS) for two years. I was also a part of Link Crew for two years, which is a program where upperclassmen mentor freshmen. I also volunteered at EV Free Fullerton, Surfriders, and other organizations around Brea. For my community service, I received the Brea Mayor's Youth Award when I graduated. During high school, I discovered my passion for math, physics, and computer programming, which is why I am now studying engineering.
